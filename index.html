Back Story.

Yes, as I mentioned in my random facts — I knew what I wanted to do by the age of 13. Growing up, I was deep into video games. But I wasn't just playing them — I was thinking about how they were built. That curiosity turned into notebooks filled with game ideas, sketches, and concepts I worked on with a few friends. Eventually, we realized: we need a website for this.

So, I started teaching myself the basics. Bit by bit, I learned how to design and build something from scratch, and I loved every second of it.

Now, as a second-year college student, I'm still on that journey — exploring design, development, and creative storytelling. At the same time, I've found that stepping away from the screen matters just as much. I'm someone who loves nature — going on hikes, camping trips, or just sitting near the water. Those quiet moments outdoors help clear my head and recharge my creativity.

Even though I'm still studying and learning, I've already picked up a lot — not just technical skills, but a mindset: ask questions, figure things out, and keep building.

I don't have it all figured out yet, and that's the best part. I'm in the middle of the process — making things, messing up, learning, and growing. And honestly, I love it.
